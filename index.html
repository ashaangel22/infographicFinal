The unfinished business
of the 1963 march
on washington

The 1963 March on Washingon for Jobs and Freedom at which Teverend Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. gave his "I Have a Dream" speech called not just for legal rights, but for jobs and a living wage. Fifty years later, four of the seven demand made by marchers have still not been met. The march is unfinished, as expanded rights without significantly expanded exonomic oppotunities has left African Americans economically disadvantaged.

Still twice as likely to be unemployed

In 2012 the black unemployment rate-14.0%-was 2.1 times the white unemployment rate (6.6%) and higher than the average national unemployment rate during the Great Depression (13.1%).
